# 📘 ISTQB CTFL v4.0 – Study Notes
## Chapter 1.2: Why is Testing Necessary?

---

### 🔎 1.2.1 Testing's Contributions to Success `[K2]`

*FL-1.2.1: Exemplify why testing is necessary*

**Testing provides a cost-effective means of detecting defects:**
- Defects can then be removed by debugging (a non-testing activity), so testing **indirectly contributes to higher quality**.
- Testing provides a means of **directly evaluating the quality** of a test object at various phases in the SDLC.
- These measures are used as part of larger **project management activities**, contributing to decisions like release readiness.

**Testing provides users with indirect representation:**
- Testers ensure that their **understanding of users' needs** are considered throughout the development lifecycle.
- The alternative is to involve representative users directly, which is usually **not possible due to high costs**.

**Testing may also be required to:**
- Meet **contractual or legal requirements** specified in agreements or regulations.
- Comply with **regulatory standards** in domains like medical, automotive, or financial services.

### 🛠 1.2.2 Testing and Quality Assurance (QA) `[K1]`

*FL-1.2.2: Recall the relation between testing and quality assurance*

> People often use "testing" and "Quality Assurance (QA)" interchangeably, but **they are NOT the same**.

| Aspect | Testing | Quality Assurance (QA) |
|--------|---------|------------------------|
| **Focus** | Product-oriented, **corrective** | Process-oriented, **preventive** |
| **Approach** | Supports achievement of appropriate quality levels | Focuses on implementation and improvement of processes |
| **Basis** | Executes tests to find defects | Works on the principle that good processes generate good products |
| **Responsibility** | Test team activities | Everyone on the project |

**Testing is a major form of Quality Control (QC):**
- Other QC methods include: formal methods (model checking, proof of correctness), simulation, and prototyping.

**How test results are used:**
- **In Testing:** Used to fix defects through debugging activities.
- **In QA:** Provide feedback on how well the development and test processes are performing.

### 🔍 1.2.3 Errors, Defects, Failures, and Root Causes `[K2]`

*FL-1.2.3: Distinguish between root cause, error, defect, and failure*

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Error (Mistake)** | Human action that produces an incorrect result; caused by time pressure, complexity, fatigue, or lack of training. |
| **Defect (Fault/Bug)** | An imperfection in a work product; can be found in documentation, source code, or supporting artifacts. |
| **Failure** | An event where the system does not perform as expected; occurs when a defect in code is executed. |
| **Root Cause** | The fundamental reason for the occurrence of a problem; identified through root cause analysis. |

**The Chain: Error → Defect → Failure**
- Humans make **errors** (mistakes) which produce **defects** (bugs) which may result in **failures**.
- Defects in early SDLC work products, if undetected, often lead to **defective work products later**.
- Some defects **always result in failure** if executed; others only fail in **specific circumstances**; some **may never cause failure**.

**Other Causes of Failures:**
- **Environmental conditions:** Radiation or electromagnetic fields can cause defects in firmware.
- Not all failures are caused by defects in code — external factors can also trigger failures.

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- Performed when a failure occurs or defect is identified to find the **fundamental reason**.
- Addressing root causes can **prevent similar failures** or reduce their frequency in the future.

### 🎯 Exam Prep Tips

- **Testing's Contributions `[K2]`:** Know that testing provides cost-effective defect detection and supports decision-making.
- **Testing vs. QA `[K1]`:** Testing is product-oriented (corrective); QA is process-oriented (preventive).
- **Error → Defect → Failure `[K2]`:** Understand the chain and be able to distinguish between these terms.
- **Root Cause Analysis:** Know that it identifies fundamental reasons to prevent future similar issues.
- **QC vs. QA:** Testing is a form of QC; QA focuses on process improvement.
