## 🔔 3.2.1 Benefits of Early and Frequent Stakeholder Feedback `[K1]`

*FL-3.2.1: Identify the benefits of early and frequent stakeholder feedback*

### Why Early Feedback Matters

- Allows **early communication of potential quality problems** before they become expensive to fix.
- Without stakeholder involvement, the product may **not meet stakeholder's original or current vision**.
- Failure to deliver what stakeholders want results in **costly rework, missed deadlines, and blame games**.
- Little stakeholder involvement during the SDLC might even lead to **complete project failure**.

### Benefits of Frequent Feedback

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Prevents Misunderstandings** | Ensures requirements are correctly understood and changes are implemented earlier in development. |
| **Improves Team Understanding** | Helps the development team better understand what they are actually building. |
| **Focuses on Value** | Allows teams to prioritize features that deliver the **most value to stakeholders**. |
| **Reduces Risk** | Enables focus on features with the **most positive impact on identified risks**. |

## 📋 3.2.2 Review Process Activities `[K2]`

*FL-3.2.2: Summarize the activities of the review process*

The **International Organization for Standardization/International Electrotechnical Commission (ISO/IEC) 20246** standard defines a generic review process framework.

> Large work products may require **multiple review invocations** to complete the entire review process.

### The Five Review Activities

| # | Activity | Purpose | Key Tasks |
|---|----------|---------|-----------|
| 1 | **Planning** | Define the scope of the review | Define purpose, work product to review, quality characteristics, focus areas, exit criteria, effort, timeframes |
| 2 | **Review Initiation** | Ensure everyone is prepared to start | Ensure participants have access to work product, understand roles/responsibilities, receive needed materials |
| 3 | **Individual Review** | Assess quality and identify issues | Each reviewer assesses quality, identifies anomalies, recommendations, and questions using review techniques |
| 4 | **Communication & Analysis** | Discuss and decide on anomalies | Analyze anomalies, decide status/ownership/actions, determine quality level, identify follow-up needs |
| 5 | **Fixing & Reporting** | Complete corrective actions | Create defect reports, follow up on corrective actions, accept work product when exit criteria reached, report results |

### Review Techniques (per ISO/IEC 20246)

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Checklist-based Reviewing** | Using predefined checklists to systematically examine work products for common issues. |
| **Scenario-based Reviewing** | Walking through specific scenarios to identify potential problems in work products. |

## 👥 3.2.3 Roles and Responsibilities in Reviews `[K1]`

*FL-3.2.3: Recall which responsibilities are assigned to the principal roles when performing reviews*

Reviews involve various stakeholders who may take on several roles. The **principal roles** and their responsibilities are:

| Role | Also Known As | Responsibilities |
|------|---------------|------------------|
| **Manager** | — | Decides what is to be reviewed; provides resources such as staff and time for the review. |
| **Author** | — | Creates and fixes the work product under review; **cannot act as review leader or scribe in inspections**. |
| **Moderator** | Facilitator | Ensures effective running of review meetings; handles mediation, time management, and creates a safe environment where everyone can speak freely. |
| **Scribe** | Recorder | Collates anomalies from reviewers; records review information, decisions, and new anomalies found during the meeting. |
| **Reviewer** | — | Performs reviews; may be project team member, subject matter expert, or any other stakeholder. |
| **Review Leader** | — | Takes overall responsibility for the review; decides who will be involved; organizes when and where review takes place. |

> Other more detailed roles are possible as described in the ISO/IEC 20246 standard.

## 📊 3.2.4 Review Types `[K2]`

*FL-3.2.4: Compare and contrast the different review types*

### Factors Affecting Formality Level

- Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) being followed
- Maturity of the development process
- Criticality and complexity of the work product being reviewed
- Legal or regulatory requirements
- Need for an audit trail

> The same work product can be reviewed with **different review types** (e.g., informal first, then formal).

### Comparison of Review Types

| Review Type | Formality | Led By | Main Objectives | Key Characteristics |
|-------------|-----------|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Informal Review** | Lowest | No specific leader | Detecting anomalies | No defined process; no formal documented output required. |
| **Walkthrough** | Low-Medium | Author | Evaluating quality, building confidence, educating reviewers, gaining consensus, generating ideas, motivating authors, detecting anomalies | Individual review before walkthrough is **optional, not required**. |
| **Technical Review** | Medium-High | Moderator | Gaining consensus, making technical decisions, detecting anomalies, evaluating quality, building confidence, generating ideas, motivating authors | Performed by **technically qualified reviewers** with domain expertise. |
| **Inspection** | Highest | Moderator | Finding **maximum number of anomalies** | Most formal; follows **complete generic process**; collects **metrics for SDLC improvement**. |

### Special Rules for Inspections

- Author **cannot act as review leader or scribe** to maintain objectivity and independence.
- **Metrics are collected** and used to improve the SDLC, including the inspection process itself.
- Other objectives include: evaluate quality, build confidence, motivate and enable authors to improve.

## ✅ 3.2.5 Success Factors for Reviews `[K1]`

*FL-3.2.5: Recall the factors that contribute to a successful review*

### Key Success Factors

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Clear Objectives** | Define clear objectives and measurable exit criteria before starting the review process. |
| **No Participant Evaluation** | Evaluation of participants should **NEVER be an objective** — maintain psychological safety. |
| **Appropriate Review Type** | Choose the appropriate review type to achieve given objectives and suit the work product type. |
| **Manageable Scope** | Perform reviews on **small chunks** so reviewers do not lose concentration. |
| **Feedback to Authors** | Provide feedback from reviews to stakeholders and authors so they can improve products and activities. |
| **Adequate Preparation Time** | Provide adequate time to participants to prepare for the review before meetings. |
| **Management Support** | Obtain support from management for the review process to ensure proper resource allocation. |
| **Organizational Culture** | Make reviews **part of the organization's culture** to promote learning and process improvement. |
| **Training** | Provide adequate training for all participants so they know how to fulfill their assigned role. |
| **Meeting Facilitation** | Ensure proper meeting facilitation to keep discussions productive and on track. |

## 🎯 Exam Prep Tips

- **Early Feedback `[K1]`:** Know the benefits — prevents misunderstandings, focuses on value, reduces risk.
- **Review Process `[K2]`:** Memorize the five activities in order: Planning → Initiation → Individual Review → Communication/Analysis → Fixing/Reporting.
- **Review Roles `[K1]`:** Know all six principal roles and their key responsibilities.
- **Author Restriction `[K1]`:** In inspections, author cannot be review leader or scribe.
- **Review Types `[K2]`:** Distinguish formality levels: Informal (lowest) → Walkthrough → Technical Review → Inspection (highest).
- **Walkthrough `[K2]`:** Led by author; individual review is optional.
- **Technical Review `[K2]`:** Led by moderator; requires technically qualified reviewers.
- **Inspection `[K2]`:** Most formal; finds maximum anomalies; collects metrics for process improvement.
- **Success Factors `[K1]`:** Remember key factors: clear objectives, no participant evaluation, small chunks, management support, training.
- **ISO/IEC 20246 `[K1]`:** Know this is the standard that defines the generic review process.
