## 🔎 Overview

Risk management allows organizations to:
- Increase the **likelihood of achieving objectives**
- Improve the **quality of their products**
- Increase **stakeholders' confidence and trust**

### Main Risk Management Activities

| Activity | Components |
|----------|------------|
| **Risk Analysis** | Risk identification + Risk assessment |
| **Risk Control** | Risk mitigation + Risk monitoring |

> **Risk-based testing** = test approach where test activities are selected, prioritized, and managed based on risk analysis and risk control.

## 📊 5.2.1 Risk Definition and Risk Attributes `[K1]`

*FL-5.2.1: Identify risk level by using risk likelihood and risk impact*

### Definition
**Risk** is a potential event, hazard, threat, or situation whose occurrence causes an **adverse effect**.

### Risk Attributes

| Attribute | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **Risk Likelihood** | Probability of the risk occurrence (greater than 0 and less than 1) |
| **Risk Impact (Harm)** | Consequences of this occurrence |

### Risk Level
**Risk Level** = measure for the risk, expressed by combining likelihood and impact.

> The higher the risk level, the more important its treatment.

## ⚠️ 5.2.2 Project Risks and Product Risks `[K2]`

*FL-5.2.2: Distinguish between project risks and product risks*

### Project Risks
Related to the **management and control of the project**.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Organizational Issues** | Delays in work products deliveries, inaccurate estimates, cost cutting |
| **People Issues** | Insufficient skills, conflicts, communication problems, shortage of staff |
| **Technical Issues** | Scope creep, poor tool support |
| **Supplier Issues** | Third-party delivery failure, bankruptcy of supporting company |

**Impact:** May affect project schedule, budget, or scope, affecting the project's ability to achieve its objectives.

### Product Risks
Related to the **product quality characteristics** (e.g., ISO 25010 quality model).

| Examples |
|----------|
| Missing or wrong functionality |
| Incorrect calculations |
| Runtime errors |
| Poor architecture |
| Inefficient algorithms |
| Inadequate response time |
| Poor user experience |
| Security vulnerabilities |

**Negative Consequences:**
- User dissatisfaction
- Loss of revenue, trust, reputation
- Damage to third parties
- High maintenance costs, overload of help desk
- Criminal penalties
- In extreme cases: physical damage, injuries, or even death

## 🔍 5.2.3 Product Risk Analysis `[K2]`

*FL-5.2.3: Explain how product risk analysis may influence thoroughness and test scope*

### Goal
Provide awareness of product risk to **focus test effort** in a way that **minimizes residual level of product risk**.

> Ideally, product risk analysis begins **early in the SDLC**.

### Components

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Risk Identification** | Generating a comprehensive list of risks using techniques like brainstorming, workshops, interviews, cause-effect diagrams |
| **Risk Assessment** | Categorizing risks, determining likelihood/impact/level, prioritizing, proposing ways to handle them |

### Risk Assessment Approaches

| Approach | Method |
|----------|--------|
| **Quantitative** | Risk level = Risk likelihood × Risk impact |
| **Qualitative** | Risk level determined using a risk matrix |
| **Mixed** | Combination of both approaches |

### How Product Risk Analysis Influences Testing

Product risk analysis results are used to:
- Determine the **test scope** to be carried out
- Determine **test levels** and propose **test types** to be performed
- Determine **test techniques** to be employed and **coverage** to be achieved
- Estimate the **test effort** required for each task
- **Prioritize testing** to find critical defects as early as possible
- Determine if **additional activities** (besides testing) could reduce risk

## 🛡️ 5.2.4 Product Risk Control `[K2]`

*FL-5.2.4: Explain what measures can be taken in response to analyzed product risks*

### Definition
**Product risk control** comprises all measures taken in response to identified and assessed product risks.

### Components

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Risk Mitigation** | Implementing actions proposed in risk assessment to reduce risk level |
| **Risk Monitoring** | Ensuring mitigation actions are effective; obtaining information to improve risk assessment; identifying emerging risks |

### Response Options to Risk
- **Risk mitigation by testing**
- **Risk acceptance**
- **Risk transfer**
- **Contingency plan**

### Actions to Mitigate Product Risks by Testing

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Select appropriate testers** | Right level of experience and skills for given risk type |
| **Apply appropriate independence** | Suitable level of independence of testing |
| **Perform reviews and static analysis** | Early defect detection |
| **Apply appropriate test techniques** | Suitable techniques and coverage levels |
| **Apply appropriate test types** | Address affected quality characteristics |
| **Perform dynamic testing** | Including regression testing |

## 🎯 Exam Prep Tips

- **Risk Level `[K1]`:** Know that risk level = likelihood × impact (or determined by risk matrix).
- **Project vs. Product Risks `[K2]`:** Project = management/control issues; Product = quality characteristic issues.
- **Project Risk Categories `[K2]`:** Organizational, People, Technical, Supplier.
- **Product Risk Analysis `[K2]`:** Know the two components (identification + assessment) and how it influences testing.
- **Risk Control `[K2]`:** Know the two components (mitigation + monitoring) and the actions to mitigate by testing.
- **Risk-based Testing `[K2]`:** Test approach based on risk analysis and control.
